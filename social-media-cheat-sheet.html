Dad's Social Media Cheat Sheet

Make a video once, post it three places. Follow the steps in order.

1 · Make & save draft 2 · Post the draft 3 · Save & post to Instagram 4 · Post to Facebook 5 · Hashtags
1

Upload your video to TikTok and save it as a draft Film with your regular camera first. A draft is a video you've loaded into TikTok but haven't posted yet. It waits for you.

Step 1

Open TikTok and tap the plus [+]

The + button is the white square in the middle of the bottom of the screen. This opens the camera.

Step 2

Tap Upload (NOT the red button)

The camera will open, ignore it. Tap the small Upload square in the bottom right corner, next to the big red record button. This opens your phone's photos.

Step 3

Pick your video, then tap Next

Your video will be the newest one in the grid (top left). Tap it, then tap the red Next button in the bottom right. You can also tap more than one video or photo here to combine them into one post and edit them together. If TikTok shows an editing screen after that, just tap Next again, you don't need to change anything.

Step 4

Tap Drafts (NOT Post)

At the bottom of the posting page there are two buttons side by side: Drafts on the left and Post on the right. Tap Drafts. Your video is now saved inside TikTok for later. Nobody can see it yet.

Good to know: Drafts only live on your phone inside the TikTok app. If you delete the app, drafts are deleted too. Post anything you really care about.
2

Come back later and post the draft Your draft waits in your profile until you're ready.

Step 5

Tap Profile

Open TikTok and tap Profile in the bottom right corner (the little person icon).

Step 6

Tap the Drafts box

In your grid of videos, the very first box (top left) says Drafts. Tap it, then tap the video you saved.

Step 7

Write a caption, then tap Post

You're back on the same posting page. A caption is the little sentence that shows under your video, like "New sea turtle painting." At the end of your caption, add a few hashtags: words with a # in front, like #oceanart. Hashtags are how strangers who love art find your video. Ready-made sets you can copy are in Part 5 below. Then tap the red Post button. Your video is now live on TikTok!

3

Download your video, then post it to Instagram Start from your TikTok profile: save the video to your phone, then put it on Instagram as a Reel.

Step 8

Go to your Profile and open your video

In TikTok, tap Profile in the bottom right corner (the little person icon). Tap the video you just posted to open it, then tap the three dots […] on the right side of the screen (below the heart and comment buttons).

Step 9

Tap "Save video"

A menu slides up. Tap Save video (a downward arrow icon). The video downloads into your phone's Photos, with the TikTok logo stamped on it. That's normal.

Step 10

Open Instagram, go to Profile, tap the plus at the top

Tap your Profile (your picture, bottom right corner). Then tap the + button at the top left of your profile page and choose Reel for a video. Careful: the button in the middle of the bottom bar is Messages, not posting, and don't tap the + on Stories either.

Step 11

Pick your video, tap Next, then Share

Your saved TikTok video will be the newest video in the gallery (top left). Tap it, tap Next, write a caption (use the same one from TikTok, hashtags and all), then tap Share. Done, it's on Instagram!

Good to know: The video will have a small TikTok watermark on it. That's fine for family and friends. If it ever bothers you, ask Megan, there are ways around it.
4

Post the same video to Facebook Same idea as Instagram. The video is already saved on your phone.

Step 12

Open Facebook and tap "Photo / Video"

At the top of Facebook, right under the "What's on your mind?" box, tap the green Photo / Video button.

Step 13

Pick the video, then tap Post

Choose the same video (again, newest, top left), tap Done or Next, write something if you'd like, then tap the blue Post button. That's all three!

Stuck? The apps move buttons around sometimes. If something doesn't look like the picture, look for the same words ("Drafts", "Save video", "Post"). And you can always call Megan.
5

Hashtags: what they are and which ones to use Optional, but they help strangers who love art find your videos.

A hashtag is just a word with a # symbol in front of it, like #art. You type them at the end of your caption. Think of them as filing labels: when you add #oceanart, your video gets filed in the "ocean art" pile, and anyone browsing that pile can find it. No spaces inside a hashtag: #oceanart works, #ocean art only counts the first word.

A few simple rules: 3 to 5 hashtags is plenty (more looks spammy), they work the same on TikTok, Instagram, and Facebook, and capital letters don't matter (#OceanArt and #oceanart are the same tag).

Any art post

#art #artist #artwork #creativeprocess #artoftheday

Ocean art

#oceanart #seaart #marineart #oceanpainting #coastalart

Animal art

#animalart #wildlifeart #animalpainting #petportrait #wildlifeartist

Painting process videos

#painting #paintingprocess #satisfyingart #arttok #watchmepaint
How to use these: Tap the Copy button on the set that fits your video. Then when you're writing your caption, press and hold in the caption box and tap Paste. Mix and match is fine too, just keep it to about 5 total.
